General Motors is an automotive company that operates in more than 90 countries in the world. A part of their process requires to ship vehicles and other parts and accessories to most of these countries. For trading purposes, the company is required to provide an HTS code to the customs autorities to identify the items in detail. Customs authorities around the world use this HTS number to assign duties and other taxes for statistical purposes. When a company provides an incorrect HTS code, the customs authorities can stop the importation process which can signify additional costs in the port, delays in the shipment and impact the overall operations. In addition to this, the company may be forced to pay fines for providing incorrect information, which can go up to millions of dollars.

I developed A Machine Learning solution that uses Natural Language Processing to support the job of the quality assurance personnel. In the previous process, the analysts were only able to review 20% of the items in a random process. With our new solution, the AI reviews all the items and provides a list of high priority items to be reviewed. Then, the analysts review only the suggested items. With this process, the analysts are now able to find 4 times more errors than before and we expect to catch 86% of the total errors with the same manpower. This does not only save money for the company because of hiring aditional personel to achieve the same level of results, but it helps the company avoid having to pay for unnecessary duties and penalties.

Project information

  • Category: Machine Learning

Quality Improvement

Quality assurance is a critical step for organizations. For this project, the company required to guarantee the quality of HTS assigned to parts in their importation process. We implemented a model that uses Natural Language Processing to recommend the most risky parts that need to be reviewed by an analyst. With the help of the tool, the analysts were able to catch 4 times more errors than in their previous model; catching a total of 86% of the total errors. With this process, the company is ensured of more than 99% in their quality KPI.
