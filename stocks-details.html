Stocks Price Prediction

New advancements in technology gave birth to the Algorithmic Trading. Traders, programmers, and researchers alike are taking advantage of Big Data from both digital and non-digital channels - they are transforming them into valuable information that complement traditional trading indicators (e.g. moving average, RSI, etc.). Furthermore, Machine Learning has allowed a purely data-driven approach in predicting stock price movements; that is, without the use of domain knowledge or theoretical trading models.

One key task in the field is the translation of news (or events) sentiments as market indicators that boost the prediction accuracies. Traditional approaches to the task include the extraction of sentiment scores from social media chatter and news articles. While these approaches prove useful, they involve a lot of steps and the use of multiple APIs. This is further complicated by defining query terms that may or may not be comprehensive for the analysis.

The limitations in traditional approaches led us to consider the Global Knowledge Graph (GKG) database, a global compilation of documents available in almost 100 languages. Each document in the GKG database has a tagged entity and a computed sentiment score. Utilizing the GKG allows us to focus on defining search queries without worrying about the APIs, webscraping, and preprocessing.

For this paper, we presented a methodology that allowed us to incorporate sentiment scores from the GKG database in the prediction of stock price movement of FAANG companies (Facebook, Amazon, Apple, Netflix, and Google). We processed GKG records from May 2017 to December 2017 using PySpark and extracted entries relevant to the companies of interest. We also filtered out non-Finance news and mean aggregated the sentiment scores by the hour. Meanwhile, hourly stock price movement for FAANG companies were obtained from Kaggle.

We were able to produce satisfying results using a Gradient Boosted Tree Regressor, sentiment scores from GKG, and other features extracted from the time series. We recommend that future studies may explore the use of other machine learning models (regressors and classifiers), and the use of deep learning models such as LSTM and Bi-LSTM in predicting stock price movements. Further, we recommend extending the time span from 6 months to several years to see how much effective GKG can be in complementing existing approaches to Algorithmic Trading.

Project Information

  • Category: Big Data, Spark, Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing
  • Project date: 2020

SUMMARY

Global libraries like GDELD GKG can increase the information of machine learning models. In this case, we used Big Data techniques to predict the value of stocks based on the sentiments of news related to FAANG stocks. Our results showed significant correlation between news sentiments and stock prices; these can be used as an external source of data to create models to predict the stock prices.
